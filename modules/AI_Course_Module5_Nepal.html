Make Any Image You Imagine — Using Only Words
⏱ 25 min 🖼️ Image Creation 💬 Prompt Writing

Lesson Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will have created your first AI-generated image using ChatGPT and will have a formula for writing image prompts that produce professional results every time.

Full Lesson

Until recently, if you wanted a unique image — a product photo, an illustration for a poster, a logo concept — you had two choices: hire a professional or spend weeks learning Photoshop. Both cost time and money most small businesses do not have.

ChatGPT (and tools like it) have changed this completely. Now you can type a description of any image you want and the AI will draw it for you in seconds. You are the creative director. The AI is your artist.

The key skill is learning how to "talk" to the AI in a way that gets the image you actually want. This is called writing an image prompt. A poor prompt gives a confusing result. A great prompt gives you something you could use immediately for your business.

The secret formula for a strong image prompt is four parts:

1. What you want to see — the main subject of the image.
2. The setting or background — where it is placed, what surrounds it.
3. The style — professional photo? Illustration? Cartoon? Flat design? Painting?
4. The feeling or quality — bright and cheerful? Clean and minimal? Warm and inviting?

You do not need all four every time — but including more detail always gives better results. The more specific you are, the more the AI understands exactly what you need. Think of it like giving directions: "Go somewhere" gives nothing. "Turn left at the tea shop and right at the school" gets someone exactly where they need to go.

The examples below show you exactly what great prompts look like for real Nepali business needs.

Important Cautions with AI Images
Watch Out For These
✗
AI sometimes makes strange errors — especially with hands and facesAI images can occasionally produce fingers with too many joints, or faces that look slightly odd. Always check carefully before posting. If something looks wrong, ask ChatGPT to regenerate or fix the specific part.
✗
Do not use AI images to deceive customersIf you create an AI image of a "product" for your shop, make sure the actual product looks similar. Using AI photos that misrepresent real products damages trust and can cause customer complaints. Use AI for creative content and atmosphere — not to mislead.
✗
Always add your own brandingA beautiful AI image with no shop name, no contact details, and no identity does not help your business. Always take your AI image into Canva and add your shop name, location, phone number, and a call to action before posting.

Here is what makes Canva special for someone running a business in Nepal. Think about that last one for a moment. A professional background removal used to cost money and take skill in Photoshop. Now you tap one button in Canva and it is done. You can then put your product on any background you want — plain white for a clean professional look, a mountain view for a tourism business, a warm kitchen for a food business. The Canva AI Quick-Start Guide
1
Create your free Canva account
Go to canva.com or install the Canva app (Android and iOS, both free). Sign up with Google or your email. Takes 1 minute. Keep your login saved.
2
Search for a template matching your business
In the search bar, type something like "Restaurant Facebook Post," "Sale Poster Nepal," or "Product Launch Instagram." Browse the results and choose a template that matches your style. Click to open it.

Replace the text with your own details
Click on any text element and type your own words — your business name, your offer, your phone number. Keep it short: one headline, one benefit, one call to action. That is the winning formula.

5
Download and share
Click the download button at the top right. Choose PNG for best quality. Download to your phone or computer. It is now ready to post directly to Facebook, Instagram, or WhatsApp — or to send to your client.

CapCut AI — Your 5-Step Video in 10 Minutes
1
Film a 1–3 minute video on your phone
Keep it simple. Film your products, your shop, or yourself talking about an offer. Do not worry about perfection — CapCut will clean it up. Bright natural light (near a window) always looks best.
2
Open CapCut → New Project → Import your video

5
Export and share

Common Mistakes to Avoid
Watch Out For These in Creative AI Work
✗
Making everything look the sameWhen all your designs use the same Canva template in the same colours, your content becomes boring and predictable. Every few weeks, change the template, try a different colour scheme, or experiment with a new style. Variety keeps your audience engaged.
✗
Ignoring comments and messages after postingA beautiful AI-made post that gets 50 enquiries but receives no replies is a wasted opportunity. The creation is only 50% of the work. The other 50% is responding quickly and converting interest into customers. Set a reminder to check comments every evening.
✗
Using the same content for every platformA Facebook post works differently from a TikTok or a WhatsApp Story. Facebook likes text + image or longer video. TikTok likes raw, real, short-form video. WhatsApp Stories like bold, text-heavy single-image designs. Adjust your format for each platform — do not just copy-paste everywhere.
✗
Overcomplicating your designsMore elements, more text, more colours does NOT mean better design. Often the simplest designs — one strong image, one clear headline, one contact detail — outperform busy cluttered posts. When in doubt: less is more. Clean space around your content makes it breathe and feel professional.

Questions About This Module

These are the most common questions students ask about AI creative tools.

"My phone is old and basic. Will Canva and CapCut still work?" +
Yes — both apps are designed to work on low-end Android phones as well as iPhones. Canva especially is very light and runs well even on phones with limited RAM. CapCut requires slightly more processing power for video editing, but it will still work — it may just take a bit longer to export the final video. If your phone struggles, the web browser versions of these tools (canva.com and capcut.com on Chrome) often work more smoothly than the apps.
"Do I need a fast internet connection to use these tools?" +
Canva and ElevenLabs work fine on regular mobile data. CapCut is mostly offline once the app is downloaded — you only need internet to access the music library and export. ChatGPT image generation does require a decent connection. If you have limited data, you can do most of your ChatGPT image work on WiFi (at home, a café, or your workplace) and then work offline in CapCut during the day. Plan your workflow around when you have good connectivity.
"Can I use AI-generated images commercially — to sell things?" +
For the tools taught in this module: Yes. Images generated by ChatGPT (DALL-E), Canva AI, and Adobe Firefly can be used for commercial purposes. You own the images you generate and can use them for your business, for client work, and for selling products. The main rule is: do not use AI to copy someone else's style so closely that it constitutes imitation of a specific copyrighted work. Creating original business content — product images, posters, ads — is completely fine and legal.
"My Nepali designs look different from international templates. Is that okay?" +
Not only is it okay — it is an advantage. Design that looks Nepali, uses local colours (like those associated with Dashain, Tihar, or Nepal's flag), Devanagari script, or imagery that local customers recognise will always outperform generic international templates for a Nepali audience. A poster that feels familiar and local creates trust and connection. Customize your Canva templates with local colours, Nepali text where appropriate, and images of real Nepali contexts. Your "local" design is your competitive edge.
"How do I charge clients more once I can offer image and video?" +
Simple and honest: show them the difference. Create two versions of their Facebook page — one week of text-only posts (what they had before) vs one week of designed image posts and a video (what you now offer). Let them see the difference in quality with their own eyes. Then present your upgraded pricing. Most business owners will immediately see the value. If they hesitate, offer one month of the premium package at the basic price — they will see the results and be happy to pay more from month two onward.
"I tried HeyGen but the free plan only gives a short video. Is it worth it?" +
The free plan gives you enough to learn and to create a few minutes of avatar video per month — which is enough to test the idea and impress potential clients with a sample. If you find that clients are interested in this service and are willing to pay for it, the HeyGen paid plan becomes a justified business expense that you cover with your client fees. Start free, prove the concept, then invest in the paid plan only once you have income from it. That is the smart way to manage AI tool costs.
