The Magic Formula: How to Ask AI the Right Way

Estimated time: 25 minutes · Foundation lesson — do not skip this

🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will know the simple "RICE Formula" for writing prompts — and you will be able to get much better, more useful answers from ChatGPT every single time you use it.

📖 Teaching Content (Full Lesson Script)

Let me ask you something. Have you ever sent a WhatsApp message that was unclear — and the person replied with something totally different from what you wanted? Maybe you wrote "send me the thing" and they had no idea what "thing" you meant? Talking to AI is exactly the same. If your question is vague, the answer will be vague. If your question is clear, the answer will be excellent.

This is the most important lesson in the entire course. Once you understand this, every other lesson becomes easy. I call it the RICE Formula. It stands for: Role, Instructions, Context, and Example.

R — Role: Tell AI what kind of expert you want it to be. For example: "You are a helpful Facebook marketing expert for a small clothing shop in Nepal." Giving AI a role makes its answers far more useful and specific.

I — Instructions: Tell AI exactly what you want it to do. Be direct. "Write a short, friendly Facebook post" is much better than just "write something."

C — Context: Give AI the background information it needs. "My shop sells handmade pashmina scarves. My customers are mostly women aged 25–45. It is the Dashain festival season." The more context, the better the answer.

E — Example (optional but powerful): If you have an idea of what you want, show it. "Make it feel like this: 'This Dashain, dress like a queen without spending like one.'" AI will match that energy.

Now, do you need to use all four parts every time? No. For simple questions, one or two parts is fine. But for important things — like writing a customer email, creating an ad, or making a product description — use all four. The better your prompt, the better your result. It is that simple.

Here is the exciting part: once you learn this formula, you will never be frustrated with AI again. When AI gives you a bad answer, it usually means the prompt was not clear enough. And now you know how to fix that.

🔑 The RICE Formula — Side by Side Comparison

Without RICE (Weak Prompt) With RICE (Strong Prompt)
✗ "Write a post about my shop" ✓ "You are a marketing expert. Write a short, exciting Facebook post for my saree shop in Kathmandu for Dashain. We have 20% discount this week. My customers are Nepali women aged 30–50. Make it feel warm and festive."
✗ "Write a customer reply" ✓ "You are a polite customer service expert. Write a kind reply to a customer who complained their order was late. Tell them the item is coming tomorrow and give a 10% discount on their next order. Keep it short and friendly."
✗ "Help me write a message" ✓ "Write a professional but warm WhatsApp message to my supplier asking for a 2-week delay on payment. We are a small clothing shop. I have been their customer for 3 years."

📋 Step-by-Step: Writing Your First RICE Prompt

  1. 1
    Start with the Role Begin your prompt with: "You are a [type of expert]..." — for example: "You are a friendly customer service agent for a small shop." This sets the tone for everything that follows.
  2. 2
    Give clear Instructions Say exactly what you need. "Write a short message / Create a list / Explain in simple words / Give me 5 ideas for..." Be specific about length too — "in 3–4 sentences" or "in 50 words."
  3. 3
    Add your Context Tell AI about your situation. What is your business? Who are your customers? What is the occasion? What problem do you have? The more detail, the better the result.
  4. 4
    Give an Example if you have one Optional, but powerful. Share a sentence or phrase that shows the style you want. "Make it sound like this: ..." or "Use a tone like a friendly aunt, not a formal company."
  5. 5
    Read the answer — and improve it If the answer is not perfect, do not start over. Just type: "Make it shorter" or "Make it more exciting" or "Can you add a mention of our Dashain offer?" ChatGPT remembers the conversation and will adjust.

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

Watch Out For These
✗
Asking only one word or one sentenceTyping "write post" or "help me" gives AI almost nothing to work with. Always give context. Even two or three extra sentences make a huge difference to the quality of the answer.

Writing Messages Like a Pro — WhatsApp, Email & Customer Replies

Estimated time: 30 minutes · Practical skill lesson — use every day

🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to use ChatGPT to write any message — professional, friendly, or firm — in seconds. No more wasting 20 minutes staring at your phone not knowing what to write.

📖 Teaching Content (Full Lesson Script)

Be honest with me — how many times have you spent 15, 20, even 30 minutes trying to write a single message? You write something, you delete it. You write again, you think it sounds rude. You write once more, and now it sounds too weak. Writing the right message is one of the most stressful daily tasks for business owners and professionals.

This lesson will end that frustration forever.

ChatGPT is an extraordinary writer. It can write messages in any tone — formal, casual, apologetic, firm, emotional, professional. And here is the beautiful part: you just need to tell it the situation in simple words, and it will find the perfect language for you.

Let us talk about the four main types of messages you write every day:

1. Customer messages — Replies to questions, complaints, orders. These need to be polite, clear, and quick. ChatGPT can handle all of them.

2. Supplier and business messages — Negotiating prices, asking for delays, ordering stock, requesting quotes. These need to sound professional but not unfriendly.

3. Personal messages — Messages to relatives, neighbours, school teachers. Sometimes in Nepali, sometimes in formal English. AI handles both.

4. Follow-up messages — When someone has not replied, or when you need to remind someone politely. These are the hardest to write alone — but easy with AI.

Here is a golden rule: When you are emotional — angry, hurt, embarrassed — NEVER send a business message without first asking ChatGPT to improve it. Emotional messages often make situations worse. AI will take your frustrated words and turn them into calm, professional language that gets results.

💬 30 Ready-to-Use Prompt Templates for Messages

📦 Customer complaint reply
"Write a polite reply to a customer who says their product arrived damaged. Apologise sincerely, offer to replace it, and thank them for telling us."
💰 Payment reminder
"Write a friendly but firm WhatsApp reminder to a customer who has not paid Rs 12,000 for 3 weeks. Keep it respectful — they are a regular customer."
🤝 Supplier negotiation
"Write a professional message to my cloth supplier asking for a 10% discount if I order double the usual quantity this month."
⏰ Delivery delay notice
"Write a short WhatsApp message to 10 customers telling them their orders will be 2 days late due to the bandh. Sound sorry and give them a small coupon."
🏫 School teacher message
"Write a polite message in Nepali to my child's class teacher asking for a meeting to discuss my child's recent low marks in maths."
🔁 Follow-up message
"Write a follow-up WhatsApp message to a potential wholesale customer who was interested last week but has not replied. Sound friendly, not desperate."

📋 Step-by-Step: Using AI for Daily Messages

  1. 1
    Decide what type of message you need Is it a complaint reply? A business negotiation? A reminder? A personal message? Knowing the type helps you write a better prompt.
  2. 2
    Tell AI the situation in simple words You do not need to write perfectly. Just describe the situation like you would tell a friend: "My customer says the cloth colour was wrong. She is upset. I want to apologise and offer a replacement."
  3. 3
    Tell AI the tone you want Add words like: "polite and professional" / "warm and friendly" / "firm but kind" / "apologetic" / "exciting and salesy." This changes the style completely.
  4. 4
    Tell AI the platform and length "For WhatsApp — keep it under 3 sentences" or "For email — formal and complete." WhatsApp messages should be short. Emails can be longer.
  5. 5
    Copy, personalise, and send Copy the message from ChatGPT. Add the person's name or any specific detail AI missed. Then send. Done in under 2 minutes — what used to take 20.

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

Watch Out For These
✗
Sending the AI message without reading it firstAI is very good, but it does not know your customer's full history or specific relationship. Always read the message once before sending. Check the name is correct, the details are right, and the tone feels true to you.

Estimated time: 35 minutes · Create a month of content in one hour

🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to create engaging, professional social media content for Facebook, TikTok, and WhatsApp in minutes — posts, captions, ads, and stories that attract customers and grow your audience.

📖 Teaching Content (Full Lesson Script)

Let me tell you something that might surprise you. Right now, the biggest Facebook pages in Nepal — the ones with 50,000 followers, the ones that seem to always have perfect posts — many of them are using AI to write their content. The secret is out. And now you have access to the exact same tool.

Social media is one of the most powerful and most free marketing tools available to every Nepali business owner today. You do not need to pay for a marketing agency. You do not need a degree in communication. You just need to post consistently — and with AI, consistently becomes easy.

Here is the hard truth about social media: the biggest reason most small businesses fail on Facebook is not because of bad products — it is because of inconsistent posting. You post for two weeks, then life gets busy, and suddenly three months pass with no posts. Your followers forget you. New customers never find you.

ChatGPT solves this completely. In one sitting — 30 to 45 minutes — you can ask AI to write an entire month of Facebook posts. That means you never have to worry about "what do I post today?" again. Every morning, you open your planned list, copy the post, add a photo, and post. Done. It takes 2 minutes.

And the posts are not generic. When you give AI good context about your business and your customers, it writes posts that feel personal, engaging, and real. Better than most people could write if they spent an hour on it.

For Facebook specifically, the posts that work best are:

• Stories about real people and real situations (like our Sunita example earlier)
• Helpful tips that educate your audience
• Limited-time offers with clear, simple language
• Engaging questions that invite comments
• Festival greetings that feel warm and genuine

ChatGPT can write all of these for you — in any language, any style, any length.

📋 Step-by-Step: Creating a Month of Content in One Hour

  1. 1
    Tell ChatGPT about your business and audience Start the conversation: "I run a [type of business] in [place]. My Facebook page has [number] followers. My customers are mostly [description]. I post in [language]. I want to create content for the next 30 days."
  2. 2
    Ask for a content calendar Ask: "Give me a 30-day content calendar. Include these types: product posts (10), tips (8), offers (5), questions (4), festival posts (3)." ChatGPT will create a full plan with topics for each day.
  3. 3
    Write 5–10 posts in one sitting Pick the first 5–10 topics from your calendar and ask AI to write the full posts. Say: "Now write the full Facebook post for Day 1, Day 2, and Day 3 from our calendar." Do this in batches.
  4. 4
    Save all posts in a WhatsApp message or Notes app Copy all the posts into your phone's notes app or send them to yourself in WhatsApp. Label them: "Post for Monday 12 Kartik" etc. Now they are ready to go — no more last-minute scrambling.
  5. 5
    Add photos and post daily Each morning, copy the day's post, add a real photo of your product or shop (always add a real photo — it increases trust enormously), and publish. 2 minutes per day. That is all.

🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will have a complete personal "AI Time-Saving System" — a set of habits and prompts that save you 2–3 hours every single day, freeing your time for family, rest, and the things that truly matter.

📖 Teaching Content (Full Lesson Script)

I want to ask you a question. What would you do with an extra 2 hours every day? More time with your children? More rest? More time to grow your business? More time for yourself? That is what this lesson is about — getting those 2 hours back.

Most people who use AI are still only using it for 10–20% of what it can do. They use it to write one post, one message. But AI can help you with almost every task that involves thinking, writing, planning, summarising, or deciding. Once you build the habit of asking AI first, you will be amazed at how much time you save.

Let me share the "AI First" mindset with you. Before you start any task that will take more than 5 minutes, ask yourself: "Could I ask AI to help me with this?" If yes — ask AI first. Then improve the result. This one habit alone can save you hours every week.

Here are the top time-stealing tasks for most people — and how AI handles them:

Summarising long documents or articles: Got a 10-page report, a long news article, or a lengthy government document? Paste it into ChatGPT and say: "Summarise this in 5 bullet points." 30 seconds instead of 30 minutes.

Translating between languages: Need to translate a product description from Nepali to English? Or understand an English email? Paste it and ask: "Translate this to Nepali in simple words." Perfect every time.

Preparing for meetings and conversations: Before an important meeting with your bank, a supplier, or a new customer, tell ChatGPT the situation and ask: "Give me 5 questions I should ask and 3 things I should be careful about." You walk in prepared and confident.

Drafting reports, proposals, or applications: Need to write a leave application? A loan proposal? A business plan outline? Tell ChatGPT the details and ask it to write a professional draft. What takes you 2 hours takes AI 30 seconds. You spend 10 minutes personalising — done.

Creating checklists and plans: "Give me a checklist for opening a new shop in Nepal." "Create a step-by-step plan for applying for a bank loan." "What do I need to prepare for Dashain food shopping for 20 people?" ChatGPT creates organised, practical plans faster than you can think through them yourself.

The "AI First" Daily Routine

Morning (5 minutes): Ask AI to summarise the day's news in your industry or area, create a to-do list for your day, and draft any important message you need to send.

During Work: Before writing any message longer than 3 sentences — ask AI to write it. Before making a decision — ask AI for pros and cons. Before a meeting — ask AI to help you prepare.

  1. 1
    Make ChatGPT your phone's home screen shortcut Go to the ChatGPT app or website. Add it to your phone's home screen so it is the first thing you see. The easier it is to access, the more you will use it. Habit starts with accessibility.

    Create a "prompt library" in your phone's notes Save your 10 most useful prompts in your phone's notes app. Label them: "Customer complaint reply," "Facebook post," "Payment reminder," etc. When you need them, open your notes, copy the prompt, fill in the details, paste into ChatGPT. Real questions from students who just finished Module 3

Do I need to know English well to use all these prompts? +
Absolutely not. You can write every prompt in Nepali, Hindi, or a mix of both, and ChatGPT will understand you perfectly. It will reply in the same language you use. If you want the reply in English, just add "Please reply in English" at the end of your prompt. Many of our best students use ChatGPT entirely in Nepali. Your language is not a barrier — it never was.
What if I try the RICE formula and still get a bad answer? +
That happens sometimes — and it is totally normal, even for experienced users. When it happens, do not start over. Simply say: "That was not quite what I wanted. Let me give you more details." Then add 2–3 sentences of additional context. Most of the time, the second or third attempt is much better. Think of it like directing an employee — sometimes they need a little more guidance.
Is it cheating to use AI to write messages and posts for my business? +
Not at all. Using AI for writing is exactly like using a calculator for maths or a GPS for navigation. No one calls that cheating. Your ideas, your business, your products, and your customer relationships are all yours. AI just helps you express them more clearly and efficiently. Every major company in the world — big and small — is using AI for writing today. You are simply keeping up.
I do not have a business. Is this module still useful for me? +
Very much so. How long before I start seeing real results from using AI? +
You will save time from the very first day. Most students notice they are saving 30–60 minutes by the end of their first week. The students who see the fastest results are the ones who complete all the exercises and use AI every single day, even for small things.
My internet is slow and sometimes ChatGPT does not load. What should I do? +
Slow internet is common in many parts of Nepal, and we understand. A few tips: Use ChatGPT during off-peak hours (early morning or late evening) when traffic is lower. The basic version of ChatGPT uses very little data — much less than watching a video. If you have a data plan, 30 minutes of ChatGPT uses less data than 2 minutes of YouTube. Also, Wi-Fi at local restaurants or hotels is often fast enough. Even with occasional interruptions, the time you save far outweighs the inconvenience.
