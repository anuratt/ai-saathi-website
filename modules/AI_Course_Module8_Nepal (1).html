Lesson 1 · Your Personal AI Audit
What AI Has Already Done for You — And What to Do Next
⏱ 20 minutes Reflection Action Planning
01
📊
The Big Idea
Before moving forward, you need to look back. The most successful students of this course do a simple audit: they count the hours saved, the money earned or saved, and the tasks they no longer dread. Seeing these numbers is motivating — and it helps you decide where to focus next.
📖
The Lesson

When you started this course, you probably had one of two feelings: curious and hopeful, or skeptical and maybe even a little scared. Both are completely normal. AI is a big change.

Now, after seven modules, you are not just someone who heard about AI. You are someone who uses it. That puts you ahead of roughly 90% of people in Nepal and most of South Asia who still have not taken this step. Sit with that for a moment.

But knowing something and using it strategically are different things. This lesson is about turning your AI knowledge into an ongoing practice — a part of how you work and live every single day.

Your Personal AI Audit — Take 10 Minutes Right Now

1
Count the time you have saved
Think of every task you have done with AI help since starting this course — writing posts, translating, researching, drafting messages. Estimate how long each would have taken without AI. Add it up. Most students are surprised — it is usually 3–8 hours already, even as beginners.
2
Identify your three biggest AI wins so far
What are the three things AI has helped you with most? A post that performed better? A customer message you were proud of? A business decision made with better research? Write these down. These are your proof points — they show you exactly where AI adds the most value for YOUR life.
3
Find your biggest AI gap
What task do you still do manually that AI could help with? What module from this course have you not applied yet? This is your next priority. Often the biggest opportunity is the thing that felt most intimidating — but now you have the knowledge to attempt it.
💡
A Honest Truth About AI Learning You will not use every skill from every module equally. That is normal. The key is finding the 3–4 AI applications that fit your specific life and business, and becoming excellent at those. Depth beats breadth. One AI skill used every day is worth more than ten skills used once.
📊
Task 1: Your AI Impact Audit

Open a notes document and write your answers to these questions honestly:

  1. Hours saved so far: Best estimate of total time AI has saved you since starting this course
  2. Best result so far: The single AI output you were most pleased with (a post, a message, an image, a plan)
  3. Biggest remaining opportunity: The one task in your work or life that still takes too long, which AI could probably help with
  4. The habit I want to build: One specific AI practice you will commit to doing every week from now on
🎯 Keep this document. It will become your AI growth journal — something you look back on in 6 months with pride.

Lesson 2 · Your 90-Day Plan
Build Your Personalized AI Action Plan
⏱ 25 minutes Planning High Impact
02
🗺️
The Big Idea
Knowledge without a plan stays knowledge. A plan turns knowledge into results. In this lesson you will build a specific, realistic 90-day AI action plan for your own life — a roadmap that shows you exactly what to do in the next 3 months to get maximum value from everything you have learned.
📖
The Lesson

Most people who finish courses feel energized for a few days and then slowly drift back to their old ways. Not because they are lazy — but because they never made a specific plan. "I will use AI more" is not a plan. "I will write all my Facebook posts using the RACE framework every Monday morning" is a plan.

Your 90-day AI plan has three phases. Each phase builds on the previous one. By the end of month three, AI will be as natural a part of your work as using your phone.

📅 Your 90-Day AI Action Plan Template
Month 1
Foundation
🔧 Build Your Daily AI Habit
  • Set up ChatGPT custom instructions with your full business context (Module 7)
  • Create your personal prompt library — save your 10 most-used prompts
  • Choose ONE type of content to always create with AI (social posts, customer replies, or product descriptions)
  • Do one AI session per week minimum — put it in your calendar like an appointment
  • Measure: by end of Month 1, AI should be saving you at least 3 hours/week
    • Month 2
    Growth
    📈 Expand to New Areas
  • Add a second AI application — try something from a module you have not used yet
  • Start using AI for one business decision per week (research, pricing, strategy)
  • Try creating one piece of visual content with AI tools (image for a post, product photo description)
  • Start a simple chain prompting project — create one complete campaign using 4–5 linked prompts
  • Measure: by end of Month 2, you should have one clear AI-powered result you can point to
    • Month 3
    Mastery
    🏆 Make AI Earn Money for You
  • Offer one AI-powered service to a client, customer, or neighbor (even free at first)
  • Calculate the total hours AI saved you over 90 days — assign that time a money value
  • Teach one person in your family or community one AI skill — teaching deepens your own knowledge
  • Review and refresh your prompt library — delete what did not work, upgrade what did
  • Set your Year 1 AI goal: what would you like AI to help you achieve by this time next year?
    • 🎯
    Make It Yours This plan is a template — not a rigid rulebook. Adapt it to your reality. If you run a seasonal business, your month 1 might be a quiet month where you build habits, and month 3 might be festival season where you use everything. The key is: write down YOUR specific version of this plan, with your own tasks.

    Lesson 3 · The Future of AI
    What is Coming Next — And How to Stay Ahead
    ⏱ 22 minutes Future Skills Staying Current
    03
    🔭
    The Big Idea
    AI is not slowing down — it is accelerating. Every month brings new tools, new capabilities, and new opportunities. This lesson gives you an honest look at what is coming so you can prepare intelligently — without stress. The people who stay curious and adaptable will stay ahead. That is you.
    📖
    The Lesson

    When the internet arrived in Nepal in the 1990s, only a few people in Kathmandu had access. Those who learned to use it first — to email, to search, to sell online — had an enormous advantage over those who waited. The same thing is happening with AI right now, today, in 2024 and 2025.

    Here is what is coming that is relevant to your life and business — explained simply, without the technical confusion.

    🤖 Coming Soon
    AI Agents — AI That Takes Action for You
    Right now, AI answers your questions. Soon, AI "agents" will take actions — booking appointments, sending emails, updating your product listings, searching the web — all automatically, based on your instructions.
    How to prepare: Get comfortable with giving AI very clear, structured instructions (which you already know how to do with RACE prompting). The same skill applies.
    🎤 Already Here
    AI in Nepali and Regional Languages
    AI tools are rapidly improving in Nepali, Hindi, Maithili, and other regional languages. Within 2–3 years, you will be able to have full, sophisticated conversations with AI entirely in Nepali.
    How to prepare: Start experimenting with Nepali prompts now. The more you practice, the earlier you will benefit as the tools improve.
    📱 Coming to Your Phone
    AI Built Into Every App
    In 2–3 years, AI will be embedded into WhatsApp, Instagram, Google Maps, banking apps, and most tools you already use. You will not need to know it is AI — it will just make everything smarter.
    How to prepare: Build comfort with AI interaction now. When it arrives everywhere, you will already understand how to get the best from it.
    💼 New Opportunities
    AI Skills = Job Market Power
    Companies in Nepal, India, the Gulf, and beyond are actively looking for people who can use AI tools for marketing, content, customer service, and operations. This is a real career opportunity — not theoretical.
    How to prepare: Start offering AI services. Even helping one local business use AI builds your portfolio and your confidence.

    How to Stay Current — Without Spending Hours Every Day

    1
    The 15-Minute Weekly AI Update
    Once a week, ask ChatGPT: "What are the most important new AI developments from the past week that a small business owner in Nepal should know about?" This keeps you current without overwhelming yourself.
    2
    Try One New AI Tool Per Month
    Just one. Spend 30 minutes exploring a tool you have never used. It might be useless for you — or it might become your favorite tool. You will not know until you try.
    3
    Teach Someone New Each Month
    The best way to stay sharp is to teach. Share what you know with a family member, a neighbor, or a colleague. Teaching forces you to truly understand what you know — and it makes your community stronger.
    🧘
    A Calm Truth About the Future It is easy to feel anxious about how fast AI is moving. Here is what is true: the fundamentals you learned in this course — how to communicate clearly with AI, how to structure prompts, how to use AI as a tool for YOUR goals — these skills will remain relevant regardless of how the specific tools change. You are not learning one tool. You are learning a new way of thinking.

    Final Questions

    After graduation — what now?

    The AI tools keep changing. How do I stay updated without it becoming a full-time job? +
    Set a simple weekly routine: 15 minutes every Monday to ask ChatGPT what is new in AI for your field. Follow one YouTube channel or newsletter (in Nepali or English) that covers AI news in simple language. And try one new feature per month — just one. The goal is consistent small steps, not exhaustive research.
    Is it too late to start using AI professionally? Have I missed the wave? +
    No — and this might be the most important answer in the entire course. In Nepal and most of South Asia, the vast majority of small businesses are still not using AI at all. This means right now, in 2024 and 2025, you are genuinely ahead of the curve. The best time to start was yesterday. The second best time is today. You have already started.
    What if AI makes big mistakes in my business communications? +
    This is why we always review AI's work before sending or posting. Think of AI as an extremely fast first-draft writer who occasionally gets facts wrong or uses slightly off tone. Your job is editor and quality checker — not to write from scratch. With practice, you will develop a quick eye for what needs adjusting. The review process gets faster as you learn what AI tends to get right and wrong for YOUR type of business.
    My children are better at technology than me. Should I ask them to do my AI work? +
    You can — but do not rely on this completely. Your children understand technology better. You understand your business, your customers, your community, and your market far better. The best outcome is to learn together: you teach them what the business needs, they help you learn the tools. But the person who understands the prompts and the context should ultimately be you — because you are the one with the business knowledge that makes AI actually useful.
    I want to teach AI to others in my community. Where do I start? +
    Start exactly where this course started — with Module 1. Gather 3–5 interested people, one phone or laptop between you is enough, and simply share what you know. You do not need to know everything — you just need to know enough to take them through their first conversation with AI and show them one real, useful result. That first "wow" moment when someone sees AI work for the first time is the spark that makes people want to learn more. You have already experienced that spark. Now help others feel it too.
