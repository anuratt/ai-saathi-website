Let me ask you something honest. When you want to post about your product on Facebook, what happens in your mind? For most people, you open the app, you look at the blank box where you are supposed to write something, and… nothing comes. Or you write something and it feels boring. Or you worry your English is bad. Or you look at other people's posts and think "How do they write like that?"

Here is a situation every business owner knows. A customer messages you on WhatsApp at 9 PM. You are tired. Your children need help with homework. But the customer is asking: "Do you have this in blue? What is the price? Can you deliver tomorrow?"

What do you do? You either ignore it (and maybe lose the sale), reply quickly with a short rude-sounding message (and maybe lose the customer's trust), or spend 10 minutes trying to write something perfect (and lose your evening).

With AI, there is a fourth option: get the perfect reply written in 15 seconds.

This is one of the most powerful ways to use AI in your daily business. WhatsApp customer service messages. It seems small, but think about this: every good message you send to a customer either builds trust or loses it. Trust is what makes them come back. Trust is what makes them recommend you to their friends and family.

Here are the five most common WhatsApp situations in a Nepali small business — and AI can handle all of them in seconds:

1. Price inquiry: "How much does this cost?" → AI writes a warm, informative reply with the price, a short product description, and a link or invitation to see more.
2. Delivery question: "Do you deliver to my area?" → AI writes a clear, professional reply explaining your delivery system.
3. Complaint handling: "I bought from you last week and I am not happy." → AI writes a calm, empathetic reply that does not escalate the problem.
4. Bulk order inquiry: "Can I get a discount for 50 pieces?" → AI writes a confident, professional reply that opens negotiation without underselling.
5. Festival greetings: Dashain, Tihar, New Year → AI writes personalized, warm messages to send to your entire customer list.

The key skill here is telling AI the situation clearly — just like you would explain it to a friend and ask "What should I say back to them?"

Lesson 03
Writing Ads That Make People Actually Buy
⏱ 25 minutes 💰 Business impact lesson 🎯 Revenue skills
03
🎯
Lesson Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will know the psychology of what makes ads work — and how to tell AI to write ads using that psychology for your specific business.
📖
Full Lesson — Read or Watch

An advertisement is not information. It is an emotion. The best ads in the world do not just tell you about a product — they make you feel something. They make you feel desire, urgency, belonging, or relief. And then they tell you exactly what to do next.

Professional copywriters — the people who write ads for big brands — study this their whole career. They earn high salaries because good writing sells products and bad writing does not. Now AI gives you access to that same skill — for free, in 10 seconds.

Here is the simple framework that all great ads follow. It is called AIDA:

A — Attention. The first sentence must stop the reader. Use a question, a surprising statement, or a bold claim. "Are you still paying too much for your internet?" "This is the softest pashmina you have ever touched." "Your khana is incomplete without this."

I — Interest. Now build their interest. Tell them something interesting about your product that they did not know. A unique ingredient. A story behind the product. A specific benefit they care about.

D — Desire. Make them want it. Paint a picture of their life with your product. "Imagine your guests tasting this pickle and asking where you bought it." "Your child scoring in the top 10 — just from a different study method."

A — Action. Tell them clearly what to do. "Order now." "Visit us today." "Message on WhatsApp." "Only 20 pieces left — don't wait."

When you use AI, you tell it to use this framework and it will write a complete ad that follows all four steps. You do not need to know about advertising. You just need to know what to ask AI.

One more powerful technique: the urgency trigger. Humans are biologically wired to act when they feel they might miss something. Phrases like "Only today," "Last 10 pieces," "Offer ends Sunday," "First 20 customers only" — these create urgency. Tell AI to include an urgency element and watch your response rate increase.

⏱ 20 minutes 📧 Email + formal writing 🎯 Professional growth

Full Lesson — Read or Watch

There is a specific type of writing that makes many middle-aged Nepali business owners feel nervous: formal letters and professional emails. Writing to a bank manager. Applying to a supplier for credit terms. Sending a formal complaint to a company. Writing to a school or government office.

This nervousness usually has two causes: weak English confidence, and not knowing the formal writing structure. AI solves both problems completely.

Here is something beautiful about using AI for professional letters: you do not even need to know what you want to say properly. You can describe your situation in very casual language — even in Nepali — and ask AI to "convert this into a formal professional letter in English." AI understands the situation and automatically applies the correct formal structure.

For example, you tell ChatGPT: "I want to write a letter to my supplier in Kathmandu. I am asking for 30 days credit instead of paying upfront. We have been doing business for 2 years and I have always paid on time. Please write a professional letter requesting this."

ChatGPT will write a letter with proper salutation, body paragraphs, and closing — with the exact right professional tone that increases your chances of getting that credit term. This is something that used to require hiring someone, or nervously asking your educated nephew for help. Now you can do it yourself, in your own time, with confidence.

The same applies to: job application letters, school admission letters, bank loan applications, complaint letters to companies, government inquiry letters, and formal quotation requests. AI knows the format for all of these. You provide the situation; AI provides the words.

You have now learned to write Facebook posts, WhatsApp replies, advertisements, and professional letters using AI. This is a remarkable amount of skill in a very short time. Now let us make sure you never forget these skills and that they keep working for you automatically, week after week.

Here is the problem most beginners face: they learn something, feel excited, use it once or twice — and then slip back to their old habits. Life gets busy, children need attention, the shop is full. One month later, they are back to writing posts the old way.

We are going to prevent that by building a system today.

The system is simple: a Prompt Library. A collection of your best, most useful prompts — saved in your phone Notes app, organized by category, ready to use in 30 seconds whenever you need them.

Think of it like a recipe book. You do not create a new recipe every time you cook dal bhat. You use your trusted recipe. Your Prompt Library is your trusted recipe book for writing. Once built, it runs your writing forever.

Your library should have these five sections:

1. Social Media: Facebook posts, TikTok captions, WhatsApp status updates for different product types and occasions.
2. Customer Service: Replies for price inquiries, complaints, delivery questions, festival greetings.
3. Advertising: Ad prompts for different seasons — pre-Dashain, Tihar, New Year, monsoon season, summer sale.
4. Professional Writing: Email formats for suppliers, banks, partners, international clients.
5. Content Ideas: Prompts for when you are stuck — "Give me 10 content ideas for a [type of business] this week."

Each prompt in your library should have [BLANK] spaces where you fill in the specific details that change each time — the product name, the price, the deadline. Everything else stays the same and gives you perfect results every single time.

This library is your business writing operating system. Build it today. Use it forever.
