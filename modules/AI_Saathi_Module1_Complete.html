What Is AI, Really? (The Simple Truth No One Told You)

Estimated time: 18 minutes  ·  PDF + Video lesson

🎯 Lesson Objective

📖 Teaching Content

Let us start with the honest truth: AI is not a robot. It is not dangerous. It is not magic. And it is definitely not only for engineers and young people who studied computers.

AI — short for Artificial Intelligence — is simply a very clever computer program that has read millions of books, articles, and conversations, and learned from all of them. When you ask it a question, it uses everything it has learned to give you a helpful answer.

🧑‍💼
Think of it like this: Imagine you hired a brilliant new assistant for your shop or office. They respond immediately, politely, and helpfully. That assistant? That is AI. And the best part: you do not have to teach it anything. It already knows.

You already use AI every single day — and you probably did not even know it. When Google Maps tells you the fastest route to avoid traffic — that is AI. When YouTube suggests the next video you will probably enjoy — that is AI. When your phone keyboard predicts the next word you want to type — that is AI. When Daraz shows you products you might want to buy — that is AI.

So you see — AI is not something from the future. It is already deeply part of your daily life. We are just going to learn how to use a more powerful version of it — one that can write, think, plan, and create for you — on purpose.

The tool we will use most in this course is called ChatGPT. You type something to it — like a WhatsApp message to a smart friend — and it writes back with a helpful, detailed response. In your language. In seconds. For free.

How to Ask AI the Right Way (The Secret of Good Prompts)

Estimated time: 20 minutes  ·  The most important lesson in the course

🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will know exactly how to ask AI questions in a way that gets you excellent, useful, specific answers — every single time. This one skill alone will save you hours of frustration.

📖 Teaching Content

Here is the most important truth about AI that almost no one teaches beginners: The quality of your answer depends completely on the quality of your question. This is the single biggest secret to using AI well.

In AI language, your question or instruction is called a "prompt." A good prompt is like giving clear instructions to a skilled worker. A bad prompt is like telling that same worker "do something" — and then being surprised when the result is not what you wanted.

👨‍🍳
The Bhansaghar Analogy: Imagine you go to a restaurant and say to the cook: "Make me something good." They might bring you anything — maybe something you do not like. But if you say: "I want a spicy chicken curry with extra gravy, not too oily, served with rice — like my mother used to make in Kathmandu" — now the cook can make exactly what you want. AI is the cook. Your prompt is the order. Be specific, be clear, be complete.

A great prompt has three things: Who you are + What you want + How you want it.

Weak prompt: "Write a post for my shop."

Strong prompt: "I am a 40-year-old woman who sells handmade jewellery in Patan, Kathmandu. Write a friendly Facebook post for my new silver necklace collection. Keep it short — 3 sentences — and make it feel warm and personal, not too formal."

See the difference? The second prompt tells AI exactly who you are, exactly what you want, and exactly how you want it. The result will be dramatically better. And with practice, writing strong prompts becomes as natural as sending a WhatsApp message.

📋 Step-by-Step: The 3-Part Prompt Formula

  1. 1
    Part 1: Who You Are (Context) Tell AI who you are and what your situation is. Example: "I am a school teacher in Pokhara" or "I am a small clothing shop owner in Butwal" or "I am a 35-year-old mother looking for..."
  2. 2
    Part 2: What You Want (Task) Be very specific about the task. Not "write something" — but "write a 3-sentence Facebook post" or "give me 5 ideas for" or "explain to me in simple words why..."
  3. 3
    Part 3: How You Want It (Format/Style) Describe the style, length, tone, or language you want. "Keep it simple." "Use Nepali examples." "Make it friendly, not formal." "Write it in Nepali." "Keep it under 100 words."
  4. 4
    Bonus: Ask for Changes If the first answer is not perfect, tell AI: "Make it shorter" or "Can you make it sound warmer?" or "Can you give me a version in Nepali?" AI loves feedback and gets better with every instruction.

Is AI Safe? The Truth About What AI Can and Cannot Do

Estimated time: 15 minutes  ·  Trust and clarity lesson

🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will feel fully comfortable and safe using AI — knowing clearly what it can do for you, what its limits are, and how to use it wisely and responsibly.

📖 Teaching Content

Before we go deeper in this course, I want to have an honest conversation with you about AI — the real truth, without exaggeration or fear. Because sometimes, people either think AI is a magical genius that can do everything perfectly, or they are afraid it will take over the world. Neither is true.

What AI is VERY GOOD at: Writing, summarising, explaining, translating, brainstorming ideas, editing, answering general questions, creating plans, and producing drafts of almost any kind of text. For these tasks, AI is genuinely extraordinary — faster and often better than we expect.

🧠
AI is like a very well-read friend who has read every book and article ever written — but who has never actually lived in the world. They can tell you how to cook dal bhat from a recipe, but they have never tasted it. They can describe Kathmandu from books, but they have never walked in Thamel. Their knowledge is vast but their real-world experience is zero. Keep this in mind when checking their answers.

What AI is NOT so good at: Getting today's news (it has a knowledge cutoff date), knowing your personal situation, making important medical or legal decisions for you, and sometimes it gets specific facts or numbers wrong — especially recent ones.

Is AI safe to use? Yes — for everyday tasks, absolutely. You do not need to be afraid of it. But use it wisely: always review its answers before acting on them, do not share private personal information, and for very important decisions (health, legal, financial) — use AI as a starting point, then consult a real professional.

Most importantly: AI is a tool, not a replacement for your judgment. You are still the decision-maker. AI just helps you think faster and work better. Your wisdom, your values, your relationships — AI cannot replace any of these. It can only support them.
